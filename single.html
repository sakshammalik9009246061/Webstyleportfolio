My Journey into Web Development

May 2026
Web Development Student Life

Web development is not just about writing code — it is about building something meaningful. I am Saksham Malik, a BCA student and aspiring Web Developer, and this is my journey of learning and growing in the world of programming.

I started my journey by learning HTML and CSS. At first, it felt difficult to understand how websites are structured, but after building small pages and practicing daily, I gained confidence. Slowly, I began learning JavaScript and discovered how websites become interactive and dynamic.

Currently, I am working on improving my frontend development skills, creating responsive designs, and learning how to make websites look premium using modern animations, transitions, and layouts. I also enjoy customizing templates and turning them into professional personal portfolios.

What I’m Learning Right Now

Right now, I am focusing on improving my JavaScript skills, DOM manipulation, and real project building. I am also exploring GitHub and learning how to deploy websites using GitHub Pages. Along with coding, I am also learning how to write clean and structured code.

Why I Love Web Development

The best part about web development is that you can instantly see your work on the screen. Every small improvement in design or code feels rewarding. It motivates me to keep learning more and build better projects every day.

"Success doesn’t come from what you do occasionally, it comes from what you do consistently."

Saksham Malik

My Future Goals

My goal is to become a professional full-stack developer. I want to build high-quality websites, applications, and work on real-world projects. I also want to explore freelancing and develop skills that help me earn through web development.

Final Thoughts

Learning web development is a journey. Some days feel easy, and some days feel frustrating, but consistency is the key. I believe that if I keep practicing daily and working on projects, I can become a skilled developer and achieve my goals.
